Rohan Padhye

Biography: Rohan Padhye is an Assistant Professor at Carnegie Mellon University in the School of Computer Science, primarily affiliated with the Institute for Software Research. Rohan obtained a Ph.D. in Computer Science at UC Berkeley, where he was advised by Koushik Sen. Complementing his doctoral work, he collaborated with Microsoft Research and Samsung Research America, developing techniques to automatically find software bugs in large-scale production systems. He is the recipient of an ACM SIGSOFT Distinguished Paper Award, an ACM SIGSOFT Distinguished Artifact Award, an ACM SIGSOFT Tool Demonstration Award, an SOSP Best Paper Award, the C.V. Ramamoorthy Distinguished Research Award, and an Outstanding Graduate Student Instructor Award.
